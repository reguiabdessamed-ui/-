Ancient Origins: Berber Kingdoms

Numidia

Capital

Cirta (Constantine)

Berbers (Amazigh)

The Algerian territory's first organized state emerged with the Kingdom of Numidia, unified by King Massinissa around 202 BCE. The indigenous Berber (Amazigh) people established a powerful kingdom that controlled much of North Africa, developing agriculture, trade, and a distinct cultural identity that persists to this day.

Roman & Byzantine Influence

Roman Period (46 BCE - 429 CE)

  • Annexation after fall of Numidia
  • Province of Africa Nova established
  • Major cities: Timgad, Djemila, Tipasa
  • Agricultural prosperity and urbanization
  • Christianity spreads in 2nd-3rd centuries
  • Berber resistance movements persist

Byzantine Era (533 - 647 CE)

  • Byzantine reconquest from Vandals
  • Limited control over coastal areas
  • Berber kingdoms maintain autonomy inland
  • Christian-Berber synthesis
  • Weakening Byzantine authority
  • Preparation for Islamic arrival

Ottoman Regency of Algiers

01

Establishment (1516-1830)

Brothers Aruj and Hayreddin Barbarossa establish Ottoman authority. Algeria becomes autonomous regency under Ottoman suzerainty.

02

Political Structure

Governed by Dey (elected leader), supported by Janissaries and corsair captains. Highly autonomous from Istanbul.

03

Maritime Power

Algiers becomes major Mediterranean naval power. Corsair activities bring wealth and international recognition.

04

Territory Control

Three provinces: Algiers, Constantine, and Oran. Beylik system for regional administration.

05

Economic Prosperity

Trade networks across Mediterranean and Sahara. Agriculture, crafts, and maritime commerce flourish.

06

Cultural Development

Blend of Ottoman, Arab, and Berber cultures. Architectural achievements and scholarly traditions.
